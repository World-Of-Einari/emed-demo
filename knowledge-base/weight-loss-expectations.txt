TOPIC: What to Expect — Weight Loss Results and Timeline

Starting a GLP-1 programme is a significant step, and it is natural to want to know what results to expect and when. This document sets out realistic expectations based on clinical evidence and typical patient experiences.

HOW QUICKLY WILL I START LOSING WEIGHT:
Most patients begin to notice a reduction in appetite and food cravings within the first one to two weeks. Actual weight loss typically becomes noticeable from weeks two to four, as calorie intake decreases and the body responds to the medication.

However, weight loss in the early weeks may be slower than expected for two reasons:
1. The starting dose of both Wegovy and Mounjaro is intentionally low to allow the body to adjust and minimise side effects. The therapeutic effect increases as the dose is escalated.
2. Early weight loss often involves fluid changes as well as fat loss, and the rate may fluctuate from week to week.

TYPICAL WEIGHT LOSS TIMELINE:

Weeks 1–4 (Starting dose):
- Appetite begins to reduce
- Mild weight loss may begin
- Side effects such as nausea are most common during this phase
- Weight loss of 1–2kg is typical

Weeks 5–12 (Dose escalation):
- Appetite suppression becomes more pronounced as the dose increases
- Weight loss accelerates — typically 0.5 to 1kg per week on average
- Side effects usually begin to ease as the body adjusts

Months 3–6:
- Consistent weight loss at a sustained rate
- Many patients reach 5–10% of body weight lost by month three
- Metabolic markers such as blood pressure and cholesterol often begin to improve

Months 6–12 and beyond:
- Continued weight loss toward the maximum effect
- Clinical trials show average weight loss of 15–22% of starting body weight at 72–88 weeks depending on the medication
- The rate of loss gradually slows as the body adapts

CLINICAL TRIAL RESULTS FOR REFERENCE:
- Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4mg): Average weight loss of approximately 14.9% over 68 weeks (STEP 1 trial, NEJM 2021)
- Mounjaro (tirzepatide 15mg): Average weight loss of approximately 22.5% over 72 weeks (SURMOUNT-1 trial)

These are averages — individual results vary significantly. Factors including starting weight, adherence to the programme, diet, exercise, genetics, and other health conditions all affect outcomes.

WHAT IF I AM NOT LOSING WEIGHT:
Weight loss plateaus are normal and do not necessarily mean the medication is not working. If you have been on the programme for several weeks without meaningful weight loss:
- Review your diet — are you eating more than you realise?
- Consider increasing physical activity
- Ensure you are taking the medication correctly
- Speak to your clinician at your next monthly review — a dose adjustment may be appropriate

IS THE WEIGHT LOSS PERMANENT:
Clinical evidence suggests that weight loss achieved with GLP-1 medications is not fully maintained after stopping the medication without continued lifestyle changes. Studies show that a significant portion of weight can be regained within a year of stopping treatment. This is why eMed places strong emphasis on using the programme period to build sustainable diet, exercise, and behavioural habits that support long-term maintenance.

HOW LONG SHOULD I STAY ON THE PROGRAMME:
eMed recommends a minimum of six months to see significant results. However, GLP-1 medications have been evaluated in long-term studies and are considered appropriate for extended use when clinically indicated. The decision on duration is made in collaboration with your clinician based on your progress and health goals.

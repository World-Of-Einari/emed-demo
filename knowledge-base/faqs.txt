TOPIC: Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I know if I am eligible for the programme?
A: You are likely eligible if you are aged 18–75, have a BMI of 30 or above, or a BMI of 27 or above with a weight-related health condition such as type 2 diabetes or high blood pressure. The final decision is made by a licensed clinician after reviewing your health questionnaire and blood test results. Start by completing the online eligibility questionnaire.

Q: Do I need a referral from my GP?
A: No. eMed is a private service and you can self-refer directly through the website. You do not need a GP referral to start the programme. However, if you have complex medical conditions, it may be advisable to inform your GP that you are starting a weight management programme.

Q: How long does it take to get started?
A: After completing your health questionnaire, you will receive a blood test kit. Once your sample is returned and processed — typically within a few days — a clinician will review your results. If eligible, your first medication delivery follows shortly after. The total time from registration to receiving your first medication is typically one to two weeks.

Q: What if the blood test shows something unexpected?
A: If your blood test reveals an unexpected finding — such as elevated blood sugar suggesting undiagnosed diabetes, or abnormal liver function — the clinician will discuss this with you. In some cases, further investigation may be recommended before starting the programme. eMed considers this an important benefit of the programme: many patients discover health conditions they were not previously aware of.

Q: Can I use the programme if I have type 2 diabetes?
A: Yes, in many cases. GLP-1 medications are beneficial for patients with type 2 diabetes. However, if you are already taking diabetes medication — particularly insulin or sulfonylureas — dose adjustments may be needed. Disclose all medications during the eligibility assessment.

Q: What medications interact with GLP-1 treatments?
A: Key interactions include insulin, sulfonylureas (glipizide, glimepiride, glyburide), and SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin). Oral contraceptives may also be affected due to slowed gastric emptying. Always disclose all medications and supplements to your clinician.

Q: Will my GP know I am on the programme?
A: eMed is a private service and your GP will not be automatically notified. However, it is generally good practice to inform your GP of any significant medical treatment you are undertaking, particularly if they manage related conditions such as diabetes or hypertension.

Q: What if I want to pause or stop?
A: The programme is a monthly subscription with no long-term commitment. You can stop at any time. If you want to pause temporarily — for example, due to travel or financial reasons — contact eMed to discuss your options. Note that stopping the medication typically leads to some degree of weight regain without sustained lifestyle changes.

Q: Is there a mobile app?
A: eMed provides a digital platform for managing your programme, tracking progress, and accessing support. The platform is accessible via the eMed app, which is available for both iOS and Android.

Q: What happens if I have side effects?
A: Contact eMed's 24/7 side effect support — no appointment is needed. Most side effects are mild and improve over time. Your clinician can slow the dose escalation schedule or provide guidance on managing specific symptoms.

Q: Can I drink alcohol during the programme?
A: Alcohol is not strictly prohibited, but it is strongly advised to limit intake significantly. Alcohol increases the risk of pancreatitis (which GLP-1 medications may also elevate slightly), can worsen nausea and other side effects, affects blood sugar levels, and contributes calories without nutritional benefit.

Q: How is the medication delivered?
A: Medication is dispatched directly to your home address, typically monthly. It should be stored in the refrigerator on arrival. eMed uses regulated pharmacy partners and tracked delivery to ensure safe, reliable dispatch.

Q: I have tried other weight loss methods before. Why would this be different?
A: GLP-1 medications directly address the physiological mechanisms that drive hunger and appetite — mechanisms that are largely beyond conscious control. Many patients who have repeatedly struggled with diet and exercise find that the medication removes the constant battle with hunger that made previous attempts so difficult. Combined with eMed's programme support, this represents a qualitatively different approach from willpower-based interventions.

Q: Are the results permanent?
A: Results are not automatically permanent when you stop the medication. However, the habits, knowledge, and lifestyle changes built during the programme can help maintain a significant proportion of the weight lost. Long-term use of GLP-1 medication is also clinically appropriate for some patients and can be discussed with your clinician.

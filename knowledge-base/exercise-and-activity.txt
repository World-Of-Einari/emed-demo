TOPIC: Exercise and Physical Activity During the eMed Programme

Physical activity is a core pillar of the eMed Weight Management Programme. While GLP-1 medication does much of the heavy lifting in reducing appetite and supporting weight loss, exercise plays a vital role in preserving muscle mass, improving cardiovascular health, supporting mental wellbeing, and making results last.

WHY EXERCISE MATTERS ON A GLP-1 PROGRAMME:
GLP-1 medications work primarily by reducing caloric intake. When the body loses weight rapidly through caloric restriction, it does not lose only fat — it also loses lean muscle mass. This is not ideal, since muscle tissue is metabolically active and helps maintain a healthy resting metabolism.

Exercise — particularly resistance training — helps preserve muscle mass during weight loss. This means more of the weight lost is fat rather than muscle, and it protects your metabolic rate, making it easier to maintain weight long-term.

HOW MUCH EXERCISE IS RECOMMENDED:
Patients do not need to become athletes to benefit from physical activity. General UK health guidelines recommend:
- At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week (e.g. brisk walking, cycling, swimming, dancing)
- OR 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity (e.g. running, fast cycling, aerobics)
- OR an equivalent combination of both
- PLUS at least two sessions per week of muscle-strengthening activities (e.g. bodyweight exercises, resistance bands, weights, yoga)

If you are currently inactive, any increase in movement is beneficial. Starting with 10–15 minutes of walking daily and gradually building up is a perfectly valid approach.

EXERCISE AND GLP-1 SIDE EFFECTS:
In the early weeks of the programme, nausea and fatigue may make exercise feel more challenging. This is normal. Lighter activity such as gentle walking is still beneficial and appropriate during this phase. As side effects ease — typically by weeks four to eight — most patients find they have more energy and capacity for exercise.

Avoid strenuous exercise immediately after injecting if you feel unwell or nauseous.

VIRTUAL PHYSIOTHERAPY:
eMed includes access to virtual physiotherapy sessions as part of the programme. A physiotherapist can:
- Create a personalised exercise plan based on your current fitness level and any physical limitations
- Help with joint pain, back pain, or mobility issues that may have developed alongside excess weight
- Advise on safe progression of exercise intensity
- Support recovery from any exercise-related injuries

This is particularly valuable for patients who have been largely inactive due to pain, breathlessness, or other physical limitations related to their weight.

EXERCISE AFTER SIGNIFICANT WEIGHT LOSS:
As weight reduces, many patients find that exercise that was previously difficult or painful becomes more accessible. Joint pain associated with carrying excess weight often improves significantly. Cardiovascular fitness and stamina typically improve even before dramatic weight changes, as the metabolic effects of the medication take hold.

TYPES OF EXERCISE TO CONSIDER:
Aerobic / Cardiovascular:
- Walking (one of the most underrated and sustainable forms of exercise)
- Swimming or water aerobics (particularly good for patients with joint pain)
- Cycling, stationary or outdoor
- Dancing
- Low-impact aerobics classes

Strength and Resistance:
- Bodyweight exercises (squats, lunges, press-ups)
- Resistance bands
- Free weights or gym machines
- Pilates (combines core strength with flexibility)

Flexibility and Mind-Body:
- Yoga
- Stretching routines
- Tai chi

MAKING EXERCISE SUSTAINABLE:
The most effective exercise is exercise you enjoy and will actually do consistently. It is better to walk 30 minutes every day than to plan an ambitious programme you abandon after two weeks. Finding activities that are enjoyable, social, or that fit naturally into your routine is the key to making movement a lasting part of your lifestyle.

TRACKING PROGRESS:
Many patients find it motivating to track their activity using a smartphone, fitness tracker, or simple notebook. Seeing improvements in stamina, strength, or distance over time can be encouraging during periods when weight loss feels slow.

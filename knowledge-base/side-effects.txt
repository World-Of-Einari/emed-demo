TOPIC: Side Effects of GLP-1 Weight Loss Medications

GLP-1 medications are generally well tolerated, but like all prescription medicines they can cause side effects. Most side effects are mild and tend to improve as the body adjusts to the medication, particularly during the dose escalation phase.

COMMON SIDE EFFECTS:
The most frequently reported side effects are gastrointestinal in nature:
- Nausea — the most common side effect, particularly in the first few weeks
- Vomiting
- Diarrhoea
- Constipation
- Indigestion and stomach discomfort
- Decreased appetite (this is also part of how the medication works)
- Bloating or burping

These effects typically peak during dose increases and improve over time. Most patients find symptoms become manageable within four to eight weeks.

MANAGING NAUSEA:
- Eat smaller, more frequent meals
- Avoid fatty, fried, or highly processed foods
- Eat slowly and stop when you feel full
- Stay well hydrated — sip water throughout the day
- Avoid lying down immediately after eating
- Ginger tea or ginger biscuits may help

LESS COMMON SIDE EFFECTS:
- Headache
- Fatigue
- Dizziness
- Injection site reactions such as redness, swelling, or itching at the injection site
- Hair thinning (usually temporary and related to rapid weight loss rather than the medication itself)

SERIOUS BUT RARE SIDE EFFECTS:
Patients should seek urgent medical attention if they experience:
- Allergic reaction: lip or tongue swelling, difficulty breathing, skin rash (anaphylaxis is rare but possible)
- Severe abdominal pain that does not resolve — this may indicate pancreatitis
- Changes in vision — particularly in patients with diabetes
- Rapid heartbeat

HYPOGLYCAEMIA (LOW BLOOD SUGAR):
Hypoglycaemia is rare with GLP-1 medications when used alone, but risk increases if you are also taking insulin, sulfonylureas (e.g. glipizide, glyburide, glimepiride), or SGLT2 inhibitors (e.g. empagliflozin). Always inform your clinician of all medications you are taking.

ALCOHOL AND GLP-1 MEDICATIONS:
Alcohol is a known risk factor for pancreatitis, and some GLP-1 medications may increase this risk further. Patients are advised to limit or avoid alcohol during treatment. Alcohol can also affect blood sugar levels and may worsen side effects such as nausea.

WHEN TO CONTACT EMED SUPPORT:
eMed provides 24/7 side effect support. Patients do not need to book an appointment to speak to someone about side effects — support is available around the clock. If a side effect is severe, worsening, or not improving after several weeks, contact the eMed clinical team or your GP.

TOPIC: Safety, Regulation, and Avoiding Counterfeit Medications

Patient safety is central to eMed's approach. The company operates as a regulated provider and prescribes only MHRA-approved medications. This document explains the regulatory framework and the risks associated with unregulated sources.

REGULATORY STATUS OF GLP-1 MEDICATIONS IN THE UK:
All GLP-1 medications available through eMed are approved by the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) — the UK medicines regulator — and are prescription-only medicines.

Licensed GLP-1 medications available in the UK include:
- Semaglutide — sold as Wegovy (for weight management) and Ozempic and Rybelsus (for type 2 diabetes)
- Tirzepatide — sold as Mounjaro (for weight management and type 2 diabetes)
- Liraglutide — sold under various brand names

Being MHRA-licensed means these medications have undergone rigorous clinical testing to establish their safety and efficacy before receiving approval.

PRESCRIPTION REQUIREMENT:
All GLP-1 medications are prescription-only in the UK. This means:
- A clinical consultation must take place before a prescription can be issued
- The prescriber must assess the patient's suitability based on their health profile
- Prescriptions cannot be issued without this assessment having been completed

eMed's clinical assessment — including the health questionnaire, blood test, and clinician review — is designed to meet this requirement fully and responsibly.

THE RISK OF COUNTERFEIT AND UNREGULATED PRODUCTS:
There has been a significant increase in counterfeit and unregulated GLP-1 products sold online, through social media, and in beauty salons. These products pose serious health risks:

Products to avoid:
- Powders in vials that must be mixed with a liquid before injection — these are not authorised forms and pose significant safety risks
- Products sold without a prior consultation with a healthcare professional
- Products sourced from social media, unverified websites, or non-pharmacy retail outlets
- Products claiming to be semaglutide or tirzepatide but lacking proper documentation, packaging, or verification

Why counterfeit products are dangerous:
- They may contain incorrect doses, wrong ingredients, or harmful contaminants
- They have not undergone safety testing
- There is no clinical oversight to detect adverse reactions
- Severe side effects from fake GLP-1 medications have been reported to UK health authorities

HOW TO VERIFY A LEGITIMATE PHARMACY:
All UK pharmacies — including those operating online — must be registered with the General Pharmaceutical Council (GPhC). Patients can verify a pharmacy's registration on the GPhC website before ordering any medication. Northern Ireland pharmacies can be verified through the Pharmaceutical Society of Northern Ireland (PSNI).

Legitimate GLP-1 medications are supplied exclusively in:
- Pre-filled injection pens (not vials or loose powder)
- Original manufacturer packaging with batch numbers and expiry dates
- With a valid prescription from a licensed clinician

EMED'S SAFETY STANDARDS:
- All prescriptions are issued by licensed clinicians following a full clinical assessment
- Only MHRA-approved, branded medications are supplied — not compounded or unlicensed versions
- Medications are dispensed through regulated pharmacy partners
- Ongoing clinical monitoring is provided to detect and manage any adverse effects
- 24/7 side effect support is available to all active patients

WHAT TO DO IF YOU SUSPECT A COUNTERFEIT PRODUCT:
If you have purchased a GLP-1 medication from an unregulated source and are experiencing side effects or are concerned about the product's authenticity:
- Stop taking the medication
- Contact your GP or call NHS 111
- Report the product to the MHRA via the Yellow Card scheme (yellowcard.mhra.gov.uk)

EMED IS NOT A MEDICAL PROVIDER:
eMed facilitates access to licensed clinicians who independently assess patients and make prescribing decisions. The clinical decisions are made by qualified healthcare professionals — eMed provides the platform, programme, and support infrastructure around those clinical interactions.

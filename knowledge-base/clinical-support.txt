TOPIC: Clinical Support, Check-ins, and Ongoing Care

One of the key features that distinguishes eMed from a simple online prescription service is the structured ongoing support provided throughout the programme. Patients are not left to manage their medication alone — they have regular contact with both healthcare technicians and licensed clinicians.

WEEKLY CHECK-INS WITH HEALTHCARE TECHNICIANS:
Every patient receives a weekly check-in with an eMed healthcare technician. These sessions serve several purposes:
- Confirming that the weekly injection has been administered correctly
- Monitoring weight and recording progress
- Discussing any side effects and providing practical guidance
- Keeping the patient motivated and accountable
- Flagging any concerns to the clinical team if needed

These check-ins are non-clinical but play a significant role in adherence. Research consistently shows that patients who receive regular support and accountability achieve better and more sustained weight loss outcomes than those managing medication alone.

MONTHLY CLINICAL REVIEWS:
Once a month, patients have a scheduled review with a licensed clinician. During this review:
- Blood pressure and other relevant metrics are assessed
- The prescription is reviewed for appropriateness
- Dosage adjustments are made if clinically indicated
- The patient's overall health progress is evaluated
- Any concerns about the medication or programme can be discussed

The monthly clinical review is where prescribing decisions are made. If a patient is tolerating their current dose well and is ready for escalation, the clinician will authorise the next step in the dosing schedule. If side effects are problematic, the clinician may pause escalation or adjust the approach.

24/7 SIDE EFFECT SUPPORT:
Patients have access to side effect support at any time, day or night, without needing to book an appointment. This is particularly important in the early weeks of the programme when side effects such as nausea are most common. Patients should not hesitate to reach out — the support team can advise on managing symptoms, provide reassurance, and escalate to a clinician if necessary.

VIRTUAL PHYSIOTHERAPY:
The eMed programme includes access to virtual physiotherapy sessions. These support patients with exercise planning, managing musculoskeletal symptoms that may accompany significant weight loss, and building sustainable movement habits.

NURSE-LED MENTAL HEALTH SUPPORT:
Weight management has a strong psychological component. Many patients have complex relationships with food, body image, and self-esteem that can affect their progress. eMed provides access to nurse-led mental health support to address these factors as part of a holistic programme.

BEHAVIOURAL SUPPORT PROGRAMME:
The behavioural support element of the programme helps patients develop healthier habits around eating, activity, and lifestyle. This is not therapy but structured coaching to help patients build the skills and mindset needed to maintain their results after the medication phase ends.

PROGRESS TRACKING:
Patients have access to a digital progress portal where they can view their weight loss trajectory, upcoming appointments, prescription status, and health metrics over time. Tracking progress is associated with better outcomes and helps patients stay engaged with the programme.

WHAT TO DO IN AN EMERGENCY:
eMed's support services are not a substitute for emergency medical care. If a patient experiences a serious adverse reaction — such as difficulty breathing, severe abdominal pain, or anaphylaxis — they should call 999 immediately or go to their nearest A&E department. eMed support should be contacted for non-emergency side effect management.

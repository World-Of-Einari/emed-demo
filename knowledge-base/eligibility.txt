TOPIC: Eligibility for the eMed Weight Management Programme

Not everyone is automatically eligible for GLP-1 medication. eMed uses a combination of a health profile questionnaire, blood test results, and a clinician review to determine whether a patient is suitable for the programme and which medication is most appropriate.

BMI CRITERIA:
- A BMI of 30 or above qualifies as clinically obese and is the standard eligibility threshold.
- A BMI of 27 or above may also qualify if the patient has one or more weight-related health conditions, such as type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol, or sleep apnoea.
- Patients below these BMI thresholds are not eligible for prescription GLP-1 medications through the eMed programme.

AGE REQUIREMENTS:
- Applicants must be aged 18 or over.
- The programme is available up to the age of 75.
- Patients outside this age range will not be accepted onto the programme.

MEDICAL HISTORY REVIEW:
A licensed clinician reviews each patient's full medical history before prescribing. Certain conditions or medications may affect eligibility. Patients are asked to disclose all current medications, as some — particularly insulin, sulfonylureas such as glipizide or glimepiride, and SGLT2 inhibitors — can interact with GLP-1 medications and affect blood sugar levels.

CONDITIONS THAT MAY AFFECT ELIGIBILITY:
- Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2)
- History of pancreatitis
- Severe gastrointestinal conditions
- Pregnancy or breastfeeding
- Severe renal impairment

These conditions do not automatically disqualify a patient, but they require careful clinical assessment before a prescription can be issued.

BLOOD TEST REQUIREMENT:
All patients must complete an eMed blood test before being considered for the programme. eMed does not accept lab results from external providers. The blood test establishes a baseline health profile and is used to make safe, individualised prescribing decisions. The at-home collection is supervised by a live virtual proctor to ensure the sample is taken correctly.

FINAL DECISION:
The final eligibility decision rests with the licensed clinician who reviews your health profile and lab results. Meeting the BMI criteria does not guarantee a prescription — the clinician makes an independent clinical judgement based on your complete health picture.

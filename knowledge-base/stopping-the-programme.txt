TOPIC: Stopping the Programme — What Happens When You Come Off GLP-1 Medication

Deciding to stop GLP-1 medication is an important decision that should be made in consultation with your clinician. Understanding what to expect when you come off the medication helps patients plan for long-term success.

WHY PATIENTS STOP THE PROGRAMME:
Patients may choose to stop for various reasons:
- They have reached their goal weight and wish to maintain without medication
- They feel ready to sustain their results through lifestyle changes alone
- The cost of the programme is no longer manageable
- They experience side effects that make continuing difficult
- A clinical contraindication develops that requires stopping the medication

WHAT HAPPENS TO WEIGHT AFTER STOPPING:
Clinical research is clear on this point: a significant proportion of weight lost during GLP-1 treatment can be regained after stopping the medication, particularly without sustained lifestyle changes. Studies following patients after stopping semaglutide show that on average, participants regained approximately two-thirds of their lost weight within 12 months of stopping.

This does not mean the treatment has failed. It reflects the fact that obesity is a chronic condition — like high blood pressure or diabetes — that often requires ongoing management rather than a finite course of treatment.

FACTORS THAT AFFECT WEIGHT MAINTENANCE:
Patients who maintain the most weight loss after stopping GLP-1 medication tend to share certain characteristics:
- They used the programme period to build sustainable dietary habits
- They established a regular exercise routine they continue after stopping
- They addressed the psychological and behavioural drivers of their previous weight gain
- They have a support system — whether through eMed's ongoing support, a GP, or personal networks

HOW TO PREPARE FOR STOPPING:
If you are planning to stop the programme, it is advisable to:
1. Discuss the plan with your clinician at your monthly review in advance
2. Ensure you have a clear dietary plan that can sustain your results
3. Have an established exercise routine in place
4. Consider whether any additional behavioural or psychological support would be helpful during the transition
5. Plan for regular weight monitoring — catching early regain is much easier to address than significant regain months later

TAPERING VS STOPPING ABRUPTLY:
There is no strong clinical evidence that tapering the dose before stopping produces better outcomes than stopping abruptly. However, your clinician may recommend a specific approach based on your individual circumstances.

RESTARTING THE PROGRAMME:
If you stop the programme and find that weight is returning, restarting is always an option. There is no clinical reason why patients cannot restart GLP-1 medication after a break, subject to a clinical review. Some patients use GLP-1 medication in longer-term cycles rather than indefinitely.

LONG-TERM USE:
GLP-1 medications have been evaluated in long-term studies and are considered safe for extended use when clinically appropriate. For some patients — particularly those with significant obesity, type 2 diabetes, or high cardiovascular risk — long-term use may be the most clinically appropriate approach. This is an individual clinical decision made in collaboration with your eMed clinician.

AFTER THE PROGRAMME:
eMed encourages patients who have completed a weight loss phase to continue to engage with the lifestyle and behavioural habits developed during the programme. The skills, habits, and health literacy built during the programme are the most durable tools for maintaining long-term results.

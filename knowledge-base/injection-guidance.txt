TOPIC: How to Administer Your GLP-1 Injection

GLP-1 medications (Wegovy and Mounjaro) are self-injectable medications taken once a week. Both come in pre-filled injection pens that are designed to be straightforward to use. eMed provides supervised injection support through weekly check-ins with a healthcare technician, particularly in the early weeks of the programme.

INJECTION SITES:
GLP-1 medications are injected subcutaneously — into the fatty tissue just beneath the skin. The recommended injection sites are:
- Abdomen: At least 2 inches away from the navel. This is the most commonly used site.
- Upper thigh: The front or outer area of the thigh.
- Upper arm: The outer area of the upper arm (this site may be harder to reach without assistance).

It is important to rotate injection sites each week. Injecting repeatedly into the same spot can cause localised skin reactions, lipohypertrophy (a build-up of fatty tissue), or changes in how well the medication is absorbed.

STEP-BY-STEP INJECTION GUIDE:
1. Wash your hands thoroughly with soap and water.
2. Remove the pen from the refrigerator and allow it to reach room temperature for approximately 30 minutes before injecting. This can reduce discomfort at the injection site.
3. Check the pen — ensure it is the correct medication and dose, and check the expiry date. Do not use if the liquid appears cloudy, discoloured, or contains particles.
4. Choose your injection site and clean the area with an alcohol swab. Allow to dry.
5. Remove the pen cap as instructed in the manufacturer's leaflet.
6. Pinch the skin at the injection site gently.
7. Press the pen firmly against the skin at a 90-degree angle.
8. Activate the pen according to the manufacturer's instructions (this varies slightly between Wegovy and Mounjaro pens).
9. Hold the pen in place for the full injection time indicated (usually around 10 seconds) to ensure the full dose is delivered.
10. Remove the pen and release the pinched skin.
11. Do not rub the injection site.
12. Dispose of the used pen in a sharps bin immediately. Never recap the needle.

INJECTION SITE REACTIONS:
Mild reactions at the injection site are common, particularly in the early weeks:
- Redness or warmth
- Mild swelling
- Itching or bruising

These typically resolve within a few days. If you experience persistent swelling, significant pain, or signs of infection (increased warmth, pus, or spreading redness), contact eMed or your GP.

MISSED DOSES:
If you miss a weekly dose:
- If it has been fewer than 5 days since your scheduled dose (for Wegovy) or fewer than 4 days (for Mounjaro), take the missed dose as soon as possible, then resume your regular weekly schedule.
- If it has been longer than this, skip the missed dose and take your next dose on your regular scheduled day.
- Do not take two doses in the same week to make up for a missed dose.

If you are unsure, contact eMed support for guidance.

SHARPS DISPOSAL:
Used injection pens must be placed in a sharps disposal bin immediately after use. Never place used pens in household recycling or general waste. Sharps bins can be obtained from most pharmacies, and many local councils offer a sharps collection service. Contact your local authority for disposal options in your area.

TRAVELLING WITH YOUR MEDICATION:
If travelling, keep your medication in its original packaging. When flying, carry it in your hand luggage rather than checked baggage to avoid exposure to extreme temperatures in the hold. You may need a letter from your clinician confirming the prescription if travelling internationally. Contact eMed in advance if you need travel documentation.
